Does the focus of collective attention cause an event to occur? A study of evidence for and against this hypothesis.

US Presidential Election 2016

We surveyed the average and total popularity index for Hillary Clinton and Donald Trump for the month before the Nov 8 election day. A higher index means greater search activity.

We found an average activity index of 44.57 for the search phrase "trump" and an average activity index of 38.36 for the search phrase "clinton".

There was also greater activity for negative search terms Trump. We found an average activity index of 47.09 for the search phrase "hate trump" and an average activity index of 43.9 for the search phrase "hate clinton".

Thus, there was a higher collective interest in the election winner Trump in general, both from people who had positive/neutral and negative feelings about him.

UK Vote to Exit the European Union 2016

We surveyed the average and total popularity index for Brexit (Leaving the European Union) and Bremain (Remaining in the European Union) for the month before the June 23 polling day. A higher index means greater search activity.

We found an average activity index of 44.57 for the search phrase "brexit" and an average activity index of 38.36 for the search phrase "bremain".

There was also greater activity for negative search terms Trump. We found an average activity index of 47.09 for the search phrase "hate trump" and an average activity index of 43.9 for the search phrase "hate clinton".

Thus, there was a higher collective interest in the election winner Trump in general, both from people who had positive/neutral and negative feelings about him.
