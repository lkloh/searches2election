Does the focus of collective attention cause an event to occur? A study of evidence for and against this hypothesis.

French Presidential Election 2017, First Round

We surveyed the average and total popularity index for the French Presidential Election candidates for the month before the April 22 - 23 election day. A higher index means greater search activity.

We searched for trends for the names of the four main candidates. We found an average activity index of 40.03 for the search phrases "Emmanuel Macron", 53.6 for "Marine Le Pen", 24.03 for Francois Fillon, and 40.03 for "Jean-Luc Melenchon". Ultimately, Macron and Le Pen won the most votes and would face off in the second round.

Thus, there was a higher collective interest in the election winner Trump in general, both from people who had positive/neutral and negative feelings about him.

French Presidential Election 2017, Second Round Prediction

We surveyed the average and total popularity index for the French Presidential Election candidates from between April 30 and May 1. A higher index means greater search activity.

We searched for trends for the names of the four main candidates. We found an average activity index of 17.37 for the search phrases "Emmanuel Macron", 23.30 for "Marine Le Pen", and a total index 495 for search phrases related to "Emmanuel Macron", and a total index of 810 for search phrases related to "Marine Le Pen".

Prediction: Though Macron won the most votes in the first round, Marine Le Pen wins the second round. Currently, news articles on May 1 show that Le Pen is surging in the polls.
